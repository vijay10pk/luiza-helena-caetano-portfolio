Not So Welcome

Monotype

10 ⅛" x 14"

This work was inspired in Adriana Varejão's Entrance Figure Il from her series Proposal for a Catechesis. The series suggests an idea that is similar to that of the Cannibal Manifesto, of culturally dissecting and owing our origins to employ artifacts that would be considered pertaining to another culture in her own art For this work, for example, referencing such different subjects (the Portuguese tradition of blue tiles and entrance figures, the actual practice of cannibalism of some tribes, and the etchings of Theodor De Bry) suggests defiance to the idea of originality or exclusiveness of some artifacts to a singular culture. It was inspired by these ideas that I decided to compose a similar piece, referencing Varejão and De Bry's work, but now incorporating a carranca and the curupira, which are Brazilian folkloric characters that evoke pride and protection from evil spirits. The final message is still similar: no culture is 100% original, not even the supposedly "pure" colonizers. Everyone is welcomed in our country, but this time I'll take my precautions
